Stephen Hawking

A tribute page

Stephen Hawking was a famous theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author who was director of research at the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Cambridge.

A timeline of Stephen Hawking’s remarkable life:

  • 1942 - Stephen William Hawking was born on 8th January in Oxford, England. This was exactly 300 years after the death of Galileo.
  • 1952 - Stephen went to University College in Oxford, same College his father attended. He wanted to study mathematics, which was not available at University College, so he pursued physics instead.
  • 1962 - In October of this year, Stephen arrived at the Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics (DAMTP) at the university of Cambridge to do research in cosmology.
  • 1963 - Stephen was diagnosed with ALS, a form of Motor Neurone Disease, shortly after his 21st birthday.
  • 1965 - Gained his PhD with his thesis titled ‘Properties of Expanding Universes’ and became a research fellow.
  • 1966 - He won the Adams Prize for his essay titled ‘Singularities and the Geometry of Space-time’.
  • 1969 - Became Fellow for Distinction in Science at Gonville & Caius College.
  • 1973 - He moved back to DAMTP employed as a research assistant, and published his first academic book ‘The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time’, with George Ellis.
  • 1974 - Stephen was elected a Fellow of both the Royal Society and Sherman Fairchild Distinguished Scholar at the California Institute of Technology.
  • 1975 - He became a Reader in Gravitational Physics at DAMTP.
  • 1977 - He became Professor of Gravitational Physics at DAMTP.
  • 1979 - Stephen held the position of Lucasian Professor of Mathematics untill 2009.
  • 2009 - Stephen was employed as the Dennis Stanton Avery and Sally Tsui Wong-Avery Director of Research at DAMTP. 
  • 2018 - Stephen Hawking died at his home in Cambride at age of 76 due to his degenerative disease. This was the day of Albert Eistein 139º birth anniversary.
